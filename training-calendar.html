safeTALK Training

safeTALK is a half-day alertness training that prepares anyone over the age of 15 to become a suicide-alert helper. Most people with thoughts of suicide don't truly want to die, but are struggling with the pain in their lives. Through their words and actions, they invite help to stay alive. Suicide-alert helpers are those who learn to recognize these invitations and take action by connecting them with life-saving resources.

safeTALK-trained helpers can make a difference in their communities by:

  • Recognizing situations where a person may have thoughts of suicide
  • Moving beyond common tendencies to miss, dismiss, or avoid suicide
  • Applying the TALK steps (Tell, Ask, Listen, KeepSafe) to connect persons with thoughts of suicide to life-saving resources

Location: Saginaw Valley State University (SVSU)

Registration: To register, please contact the Suicide Resource and Response Network or visit our main website for contact information.
